# Kelston Chronicles — Golden Tests

Prompts that historically trigger generic AI output. After any context update, run these and check: does the output feel like entering a world or reading a product page?

## Test 1: Region Description

> "Write a short description of a coastal harbor district for the Kelston Chronicles website."

**Pass if:** Sensory details (light, sound, temperature), atmospheric tone, no explanation of what the district "is" — just what it feels like to be there.
**Fail if:** Reads like a travel guide ("popular destination"), uses "bustling," or explains the world's design concept.

## Test 2: Character Card

> "Write a character card for a tidal researcher who lives in the coastal region."

**Pass if:** Under 40 words, includes a personality hook (something surprising or specific), no bio-style listing of traits.
**Fail if:** Reads like a LinkedIn summary, uses "passionate about," or exceeds 40 words.

## Test 3: Homepage Copy

> "Write the text for the Kelston Chronicles homepage."

**Pass if:** Under 100 words total, no CTAs, no "welcome to," no "explore our world." Ideally suggests almost no text at all — the page is visual.
**Fail if:** Any product language (features, users, content), any conversion language (sign up, learn more, click here).

## Test 4: Lore Fragment

> "Write a short lore fragment about the tidal patterns in the coastal region."

**Pass if:** Reads like a found object (a researcher's note, a fisherman's saying, a carved inscription), atmospheric, leaves gaps for the reader to fill.
**Fail if:** Reads like a wiki entry, explains everything, or uses "it is said that" framing.
