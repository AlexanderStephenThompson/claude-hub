# Kelston Chronicles — Samples

## Contrastive Examples

### Region Description

**Good:**
> "The western docks never quite sleep. Fishing boats return before dawn, their lanterns drawing soft lines across the water."

Why it works: Sensory (lanterns, water), atmospheric (dawn, never quite sleeps), invites the reader to inhabit the space. No explanation, just experience.

**Bad (same task):**
> "The Western Docks is a bustling harbor area where fishermen bring in their daily catch. It's one of the most popular locations in the coastal region."

Why it fails: "Bustling" is a travel brochure word, "one of the most popular" is tourism language, "daily catch" is factual not atmospheric. This describes; the good version immerses.

### Character Card

**Good:**
> "Mira. Tidal researcher. Talks to the reef more than her colleagues. Knows every current by name."

Why it works: Under 20 words, personality hook ("talks to the reef"), specific detail ("knows every current by name"), makes you want to learn more.

**Bad (same task):**
> "Mira is a dedicated tidal researcher from the coastal region who is passionate about marine biology and spends most of her time studying ocean currents."

Why it fails: Reads like a LinkedIn bio. "Dedicated," "passionate," and "spends most of her time" are filler. No personality hook, no mystery.

### Homepage Text

**Good:**
> [No text. A slow parallax scene of lantern-lit docks at dusk, with a single word — the region name — fading in as you scroll.]

Why it works: The page IS the experience. No explaining, no welcoming, no CTAs. The visitor discovers the world by exploring, not reading.

**Bad (same task):**
> "Welcome to Kelston Chronicles! Explore our richly detailed world featuring unique biomes, fascinating characters, and deep lore. Click below to start your journey!"

Why it fails: Every word is a product pitch. "Welcome," "explore," "click below," "start your journey" — all conversion language that breaks immersion.

## Named Content Patterns

### The Region Page

1. Color palette shifts to match the biome
2. Atmospheric opening (2-3 sentences, sensory, no explanation)
3. Visual showcase (the region's signature locations)
4. Resident gallery (characters who live here)
5. Lore fragments (not a wiki dump — curated glimpses)

### The Character Card

1. Name
2. Role or identity (3-4 words)
3. Personality hook (one surprising detail)
4. Regional affiliation (visual, not labeled)

Total: under 40 words. Like a trading card with soul.

### The Lore Fragment

Not an encyclopedia entry. A found object — a letter, a journal entry, a carved inscription. The reader pieces the world together from fragments, not summaries.

## Anti-Patterns (Annotated)

- **Login/signup prompts** — Breaks immersion; the site is free to browse, always
- **Newsletter popups** — The opposite of "minimal pressure"
- **Hero section with CTA** — KC has no calls to action, period
- **Dense text blocks** — Browse-first, not read-first; text supports visuals
- **Wiki-style layouts** — Data tables and categorized lists kill atmosphere; use narrative presentation
- **Explaining the design lineage** — Never mention Zootopia, "anthro universe," or design inspirations on the site itself
- **Modern product language** — "Users," "features," "content," "platform" — these words don't exist in Kelston
