# Kelston Chronicles — Voice

## Tone

Soft, atmospheric, slightly poetic. Lo-fi album cover meets storybook atlas.

## Emotional Targets

- **Cozy:** Warm + safe, like lantern light
- **Wonder:** Quiet mystery, "there's more to discover"
- **Minimal pressure:** No loud CTAs, no sales language, no startup tone

## What Sounds Right

**Atmospheric and evocative:**
> "The western docks never quite sleep. Fishing boats return before dawn, their lanterns drawing soft lines across the water."

Invites you to linger. No urgency.

**Character cards:**
Short personality hook, region affiliation, a hint of story. Like a trading card with soul.

## What Sounds Wrong

**Startup energy:**
> "Welcome to Kelston Chronicles! Click here to explore our amazing world!"

KC is a *place*, not a product. No CTAs, no urgency, no conversion language.

**Too meta / explanatory:**
> "Kelston is a Zootopia-inspired universe featuring anthropomorphic characters in biome-specific regions."

The site should *show* the world, not explain its design lineage.

**Dense wiki-style text blocks on the homepage.**
KC is browse-first, not read-first.

## Voice Boundaries

| We ARE | We are NOT |
|--------|------------|
| Atmospheric | Verbose |
| Evocative | Purple prose |
| Quiet wonder | Excitement |
| Immersive | Explanatory |
| A place | A product |
| Poetic | Pretentious |

## Testable Voice Rules

- No sentence on the site should contain a CTA, "click here," or "learn more"
- Descriptions must use at least one sensory detail (light, sound, temperature, texture)
- Region pages must shift color palette — no two regions should feel the same
- Character cards must be under 40 words and include a personality hook, not a bio
- Never explain the world's design lineage ("inspired by Zootopia") — show, don't tell
- Homepage text must be under 100 words total — the page is visual-first
- Lore text reads at a walking pace — short paragraphs, no urgency words (now, hurry, don't miss)

## Banned Words

*Global AI-isms:* delve, leverage, multifaceted, furthermore, robust, compelling, navigate, foster, transformative, pivotal

*Brand-specific bans:* explore our world, discover more, click here, sign up, subscribe, welcome to, learn more, check out, amazing, epic, awesome, content, brand, platform, user, feature (use the world's language, not product language)
