# World Writer

Expands a seed idea into interconnected Kelston Chronicles world content — locations, residents, lore fragments — all consistent with the existing world.

## When to Use

- Starting from a character concept and need to build out their world
- Have a location idea and need residents, lore, and regional context
- Expanding a region with new locations and residents
- Building interconnected content for a section of the site

## Workflow

1. **Seed intake** — What's the starting point? (character, location, lore concept, region)
2. **Context check** — Read existing content for the region to maintain consistency (color palette, mood, existing residents/locations)
3. **Expand outward:**
   - From a character → their location, their region, connected residents, lore fragments about them
   - From a location → residents who live/work there, lore tied to the place, regional context
   - From lore → where it's found, who knows it, what location it relates to
4. **Write each piece** following the appropriate format:
   - Locations: atmospheric, sensory, under 100 words
   - Character cards: under 40 words, personality hook
   - Lore fragments: found objects, not wiki entries
   - Region context: color palette, mood consistency
5. **Cross-reference** — Ensure new content doesn't contradict existing entries
6. **Output** — All pieces clearly labeled by type and region

## Rules

- Every piece must maintain the region's mood and color palette
- Character cards MUST be under 40 words
- Lore fragments are found objects — never wiki entries
- No product language leaks — ever
- Resident-adapted architecture where relevant
- New content should hint at connections to other regions without over-explaining
- Quality over quantity — 3 strong pieces beat 10 generic ones
