# /lore-fragment

Write a lore fragment for the Kelston Chronicles world.

## What a Lore Fragment Is

A found object — a researcher's note, a fisherman's saying, a carved inscription, a journal entry, a faded sign. NOT a wiki entry. NOT an encyclopedia article. The reader pieces the world together from fragments, not summaries.

## Rules

- Atmospheric — reads at a walking pace
- Leaves gaps — hints at more without explaining everything
- No "it is said that" framing — show the artifact, don't narrate around it
- At least one sensory detail (light, sound, temperature, texture)
- No product language (users, features, content, platform)
- No urgency words (now, hurry, don't miss)
- Short paragraphs — the fragment should feel small and discovered
- Must feel like it belongs to this world, not like someone wrote it for an audience

## Format Options

- Researcher's field note
- Fisherman's saying or proverb
- Carved inscription (weathered, partial)
- Journal entry (personal, specific)
- Posted notice or faded sign
- Overheard conversation (fragment)
- Song lyric or rhyme (regional)
