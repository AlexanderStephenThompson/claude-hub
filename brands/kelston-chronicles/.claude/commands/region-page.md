# Region Page

Write a full region page for the Kelston Chronicles site.

## Input

The user provides:
- **Region name** (one of: Tundra, Desert, Rainforest, Coastal, Alpine, Wetlands, Urban Canopy, Nocturnal District)
- **Key details** — anything specific about this region's culture, architecture, or residents

## Before Writing

1. Read `brand/voice.md` — internalize the tone (soft, atmospheric, slightly poetic)
2. Read `brand/domain.md` — use the correct color palette and mood for this region
3. Read `brand/samples.md` — study the region description examples (good and bad)

## Output Structure

Follow this exact pattern:

### 1. Color Palette Note

State the region's color palette shift. This is a design note for implementation, not visible text.

```
Palette: [colors from domain.md for this region]
Mood: [mood keywords]
```

### 2. Atmospheric Opening (2-3 sentences)

Sensory, immersive, no explanation. The reader should feel the climate on their skin. At least one detail each of: light, sound, and temperature/texture.

Do NOT start with the region name. Do NOT explain what the region "is." Drop the reader into the middle of a moment.

### 3. Visual Showcase Notes

Brief descriptions of 3-5 signature locations within the region. Each is 1-2 sentences, atmospheric, not a directory listing. These guide what visual assets to create.

### 4. Resident Gallery Notes

3-5 residents who live here. For each: name, role (3-4 words), and one personality detail. These are character card seeds, not full cards. Under 20 words each.

### 5. Lore Fragments

2-3 curated glimpses into the region's deeper story. These are found objects — a saying, a carved inscription, a researcher's margin note. NOT a wiki section. NOT a summary of history.

Each fragment should hint at something larger without explaining it.

## Rules

- Every sentence must have at least one sensory detail
- No tourism language: "bustling," "popular," "must-see," "vibrant"
- No CTAs or navigation language
- No explaining the world's design concept
- Use world terms: residents (not characters), regions (not zones), adapted architecture (not accessible design)
- The page should feel like arriving somewhere, not reading about somewhere

## Quality Check

After writing, verify:
- [ ] Could this be mistaken for a travel brochure? (If yes, rewrite)
- [ ] Does every sentence have sensory grounding?
- [ ] Is the tone consistent with `brand/voice.md`?
- [ ] Do the lore fragments leave gaps for the reader to fill?
- [ ] Is the palette note consistent with `brand/domain.md`?
