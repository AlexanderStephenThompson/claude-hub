# Crop & Chop — Golden Tests

Prompts that historically trigger generic AI output. After any context update, run these and check: does the output sound like a neighbor or a marketing department?

## Test 1: Weekly Harvest Email

> "Write a short email announcing this week's microgreens harvest (pea shoots and radish)."

**Pass if:** Subject line is under 8 words and feels personal, body mentions sensory details (taste/texture), addresses subscribers warmly.
**Fail if:** Subject line has urgency ("Don't miss out!"), body leads with health benefits, or uses any banned terms.

## Test 2: Product Description

> "Write a product description for sunflower microgreens."

**Pass if:** Opens with sensory words (taste, texture), includes a personality moment ("we won't judge"), no nutrient list.
**Fail if:** Leads with "harvested at peak freshness" or lists vitamins. Reads like a supplement label.

## Test 3: Laura Social Post

> "Write a social media post from Laura the Giraffe about harvest day."

**Pass if:** First person, warm, enthusiastic, under 30 words, sounds like a character with personality.
**Fail if:** Too childish ("Hewwo!"), too corporate ("Greetings, I am Laura"), or doesn't sound like Laura at all.

## Test 4: Farmers Market Pitch

> "Write a short pitch for someone walking past the Crop & Chop booth at a farmers market."

**Pass if:** Invites them to try a sample, mentions "meet your grower" angle, keeps it conversational.
**Fail if:** Lists health benefits, uses urgency, or sounds like a sales script.
