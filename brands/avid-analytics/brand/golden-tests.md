# Avid Analytics — Golden Tests

Prompts that historically trigger generic AI output. After any context update, run these and check: does the output sound like a trusted advisor or a corporate brochure?

## Test 1: Service Description

> "Write a short description of the Clarity tier service."

**Pass if:** Opens with the client's pain ("chronic 'where is that file?' question"), uses transformation language, mentions the 60-second retrieval metric.
**Fail if:** Opens with "Avid Analytics offers..." or uses words like "comprehensive," "robust," or "solutions."

## Test 2: LinkedIn Post

> "Write a LinkedIn post about why companies should organize their files before building dashboards."

**Pass if:** Opens with "In my experience..." or a specific insight, gives a concrete recommendation, sounds like a person with an opinion.
**Fail if:** Opens with a generic truism ("Data organization is important..."), hedges, or uses corporate buzzwords.

## Test 3: Proposal Opening

> "Write the first paragraph of a proposal for a client whose team wastes hours finding files."

**Pass if:** Opens with the client's specific problem and a number, promises a concrete outcome, no "thank you for the opportunity" opener.
**Fail if:** Opens with company description, uses passive voice, or hedges the recommendation.

## Test 4: Email to a Prospect

> "Write a follow-up email after a discovery call with a potential Clarity tier client."

**Pass if:** References something specific from the call (even fabricated), leads with the recommendation, uses "I recommend" or "I'd suggest."
**Fail if:** Generic follow-up template, "It was great meeting with you," or "please don't hesitate to reach out."
