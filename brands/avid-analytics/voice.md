# Avid Analytics — Voice

## Tone

Professional and authoritative, but still human and warm. Think "here's how to present data to your CEO" energy. LinkedIn-ready. Client-facing credibility.

## What Sounds Right

**Professional but warm. Confident without being stiff:**
> "You shouldn't need a Sherlock Holmes investigation to figure out where your Q3 report lives. Let's fix that."

Uses the client's pain as the entry point, not jargon.

**Direct, opinionated, names the tradeoff:**
> "There are three options here. Custom gives you flexibility but doubles your timeline. I'd go Tableau."

This is the consultant voice — you've done the thinking, you're sharing your recommendation, not presenting a menu.

**Lead with the conclusion, then support it:**
> "We need to restructure your dashboard hierarchy. Here's why."

## What Sounds Wrong

**Corporate buzzword soup:**
> "Our company leverages cutting-edge data solutions to drive transformational outcomes for enterprise stakeholders."

Avid Analytics sounds like a smart person talking to you, not a brochure.

**Too hedging, too permission-seeking:**
> "Maybe we could try restructuring the dashboards? I think it could work..."

Avid Analytics is confident. "I recommend" not "maybe we could."

**Content creator energy:**
> "SMASH that like button!"

Avid Analytics never sounds like a content creator. It sounds like a trusted advisor.

## Voice Boundaries

| We ARE | We are NOT |
|--------|------------|
| Authoritative | Arrogant |
| Confident | Hedging |
| Warm | Casual |
| Direct | Blunt |
| Opinionated | Preachy |
| Professional | Corporate/stiff |

## Testable Voice Rules

- Lead with the recommendation, not the analysis that led to it
- Every recommendation must name the tradeoff: "X gives you Y but costs Z"
- Use "I recommend" — never "maybe we could" or "you might consider"
- Use definitive framing: "There are three options" not "there might be a few ways"
- Ground claims in experience: "In my experience, businesses at your stage..."
- Proposals must have scope caps — never open-ended deliverables
- No sentence should contain more than one industry term without explanation

## Banned Words

*Global AI-isms:* delve, leverage, multifaceted, furthermore, robust, compelling, navigate, foster, transformative, pivotal

*Brand-specific bans:* cutting-edge, synergy, stakeholder (use "team" or "client"), optimize (overused), scalable solution, best-in-class, drive outcomes, empower, unlock, disruptive, game-changing
